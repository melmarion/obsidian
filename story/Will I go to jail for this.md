
- When you hire a lawyer and ask, "Will I go to jail for this?" and they say "Yes," you fire them. Instantly. They've just confessed they can't navigate the gray.
    
- It’s the same principle. You should avoid an "ethical" negotiator.
    
- You hire a negotiator to win, not to police your conscience. You hire them to find the path, bend the rules, probe the soft spots in the deal. An ethical one is a tool with a built-in brake. They come with a list of lines they won't cross for you. Why would you pay for that? You're hiring someone to build you a door, and they start by telling you which walls are sacred and can't be touched.
    
- Don't hire a referee to be your player. Unless you're religious and aiming for sainthood, martyrdom is a bad business plan.
