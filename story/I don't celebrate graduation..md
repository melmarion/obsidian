
- I don't celebrate graduation. I'm not proud of an accomplishment that's just following a map everyone else gets.
    
- I only celebrate a genuine win. Something earned, not given. Something difficult.
    
- I don't even celebrate my birthday. Why would I? I didn't fight to be born. I didn't earn it. Throwing a party for something you just woke up with feels hollow. I'd rather save the champagne for the day I win a brutal lawsuit. For the day I close the deal that broke everyone else. For a victory that demanded something from me.
    
- Being alive isn't the achievement. It's the default setting. You don't get a trophy for showing up. Not when you know, deep down, you were built to do more than just occupy space. Celebration is fuel. You save it for the climbs that actually test you, for the finish lines you had to claw across. Everything else is just a blunder.
