
- People have a natural wall up against strangers. It's a default setting. And vagueness? The brain hates a vacuum. It fills the empty space with the worst possible story.
    
- Put those two facts together. The conclusion is simple, but brutal.
    
- When you reach out, you cannot be vague. You are a stranger asking their brain to do extra work. You will lose. Be blunt. Cut through.
    
- Then, immediately, you offer the other side of the scale: "In return, here is what I can do for you." Name it. Be specific. You are not asking for a favor. You are proposing a transaction. You respect their time by stating your need, and you respect their intelligence by stating the value upfront.
    
- You're not just contacting a person. You're hacking two core human instincts: distrust of the unknown, and the need to resolve uncertainty. Give them a clear, fair deal, and you give their brain a reason to open the gate.
