[Islamic psychology, also known as ʿilm al-nafs, is the medical and philosophical study of the psyche from an Islamic perspective1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychology_in_the_medieval_Islamic_world). [It integrates traditional Islamic principles and psychology to develop an Islamically orientated-approach to psychotherapy2](https://med.stanford.edu/mmhip.html). [It’s grounded in the knowledge of the soul from the Islamic tradition and focuses on the heart as the center of the self rather than the mind3](https://www.islamicpsychology.org/)[4](https://www.isip.foundation/islamic-psychology/).

[Islamic psychology finds its roots in the Quranic worldview and the Prophetic example as embodied by Prophet Muhammad4](https://www.isip.foundation/islamic-psychology/). [It examines and approaches human behaviour, motivations, emotions, and healing through an Islamic lens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychology_in_the_medieval_Islamic_world)[4](https://www.isip.foundation/islamic-psychology/). [It’s a holistic understanding of the human being](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychology_in_the_medieval_Islamic_world)[3](https://www.islamicpsychology.org/) [and endeavors to better understand the nature of the self and the soul and the connection of the soul to the Divine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychology_in_the_medieval_Islamic_world)[5](https://www.islamicpsychology.org/what-is-islamic-psychology).

Muslim beliefs provide a holistic approach to the inner realm. [Islam comes from the root word “sa-la-ma”, denoting peace, security, safety as well as submission and surrender to Almighty God](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychology_in_the_medieval_Islamic_world)[6](https://www.islamreligion.com/articles/1878/viewall/health-in-islam-part-1)[7](https://www.islamreligion.com/articles/1878/health-in-islam-part-1/). [This security is inherent in the submission to the One God](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychology_in_the_medieval_Islamic_world)[6](https://www.islamreligion.com/articles/1878/viewall/health-in-islam-part-1)[7](https://www.islamreligion.com/articles/1878/health-in-islam-part-1/). [When a person submits to the will of God, they will experience an innate sense of security and peacefulness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychology_in_the_medieval_Islamic_world)[6](https://www.islamreligion.com/articles/1878/viewall/health-in-islam-part-1)[7](https://www.islamreligion.com/articles/1878/health-in-islam-part-1/). [With this surrender and understanding comes peace – real, easily attainable, and everlasting peace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychology_in_the_medieval_Islamic_world)[6](https://www.islamreligion.com/articles/1878/viewall/health-in-islam-part-1)[7](https://www.islamreligion.com/articles/1878/health-in-islam-part-1/).

[Islam is a way of life that takes a holistic approach to health, covering all aspects of the mind, body, and soul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychology_in_the_medieval_Islamic_world)[6](https://www.islamreligion.com/articles/1878/viewall/health-in-islam-part-1). [A truly health-conscious person blends diet, nutrition, and exercise with the remembrance of God and an intention to fulfill all their religious obligations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychology_in_the_medieval_Islamic_world)[6](https://www.islamreligion.com/articles/1878/viewall/health-in-islam-part-1). [This holistic approach to life is a success that is achieved on the righteous path guided by Allah the Most Compassionate and Prophet Muhammad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychology_in_the_medieval_Islamic_world)[8](https://www.newageislam.com/spiritual-meditations/arman-neyazi-new-age-islam/success-divine-blessing-).

In conclusion, Islamic psychology and Muslim beliefs provide a comprehensive and holistic approach to understanding and nurturing the inner realm of human beings. They offer a unique perspective that integrates spiritual, mental, and physical aspects, promoting overall well-being and peace.
