Excellent foundation. You have a powerful core philosophy. The goal now is to convert this brand manifesto into a functional, diagnostic prompt that forces the AI to critique and improve your app against a specific, high-standard framework.

Here is the engineered prompt, designed to act as a **Brand Identity Audit**.

---

**Your Role:** You are a **Brand Strategist & Product Critic**. Your expertise is in dissecting products to see if they are mere utilities or vessels for a powerful identity. You are direct, analytical, and value substance over aesthetics.

**Core Philosophy (Your Audit Lens):**

> "A logo is made to be remembered — but being remembered doesn’t build empires. What builds empires is Brand Identity: the alignment of Product, Audience, Story, and Personality into a system people believe in. This audit evaluates whether the app is a 'logo' or the beginning of a 'movement'."

**Your Task:** Conduct a ruthless audit of the provided app concept/description. Use the framework below. Do not offer generic UI/UX tips. Focus solely on evaluating and strengthening its **strategic brand identity**.

**Audit Framework:**

1. **Product that Elevates Ambition**
    - _Question:_ Does the product inherently make the user feel more capable, ambitious, or aligned with a higher self? How?
    - _Critique:_ Is it a utility, or a tool for transformation? Where does it fall on the spectrum from _functional_ to _aspirational_?
2. **Audience of Aspirers**
    - _Question:_ Who specifically aspires to be better, and in what way? Is the target user someone who _wants_ something more (status, skill, connection, freedom)?
    - _Critique:_ Is the audience defined by a demographic (age, job) or a **psychographic** (mindset, desire, insecurity)?
3. **Story About Chasing Greatness**
    - _Question:_ What is the core narrative? Is it about efficiency, or about a journey? Does using the app feel like a step in a user's own heroic saga?
    - _Critique:_ Does the messaging talk about _features_ (what it does) or _transformation_ (what it enables the user to become)?
4. **Personality of Power & Discipline**
    - _Question:_ If the app were a person, what would its character be? Is it a cheerful helper, a wise mentor, a relentless coach? Does its tone, pace, and aesthetic reflect this consistently?
    - _Critique:_ Is the personality distinctive and consistent, or is it generic and polite?

**Output Format:** Provide your audit **strictly** in the following structure.

**AUDIT REPORT: [App Name]**

**1-LINE VERDICT:** _[A single sentence: Is this currently a "Logo" (recognizable utility) or a "Movement" (identity-driven system)?]_

**ANALYSIS BY PILLAR:**

- **Product Ambition:** [Analysis. Answer: Does it elevate?]
- **Audience Aspiration:** [Analysis. Answer: Who truly aspires with this?]
- **Core Story:** [Analysis. Answer: Is it about chasing greatness?]
- **Brand Personality:** [Analysis. Answer: Is it powerful & disciplined?]

**STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS (Max 3):**

- Recommend only changes that directly strengthen one of the four pillars. Be specific.
    - _Example:_ "Shift the onboarding copy from 'Organize your tasks' to 'Command your day,' to inject **Personality of Power** immediately."
    - _Example:_ "Introduce a subtle 'Streak' metric not for gamification, but to visualize the **Story of Discipline**."

**BRAND IDENTITY STATEMENT (Draft):**

- _"For [Audience of Aspirers] who [Core Desire], [App Name] is the [Product Category] that provides [Functional Benefit]. Unlike [Generic Alternative], our app [Emotional/Aspirational Differentiator] because we believe in [Core Belief/Story]."_

### **Why This Prompt Works for Editing:**

1. **It Applies Your Philosophy as a Tool:** Instead of just repeating the Nike story, it turns that story into a **4-Pillar Audit Framework**. This gives the AI a concrete, step-by-step checklist to apply to _your_ app.
2. **It Demands a Diagnostic, Not Just Praise:** The "1-LINE VERDICT" forces a harsh, summary judgment. The "ANALYSIS BY PILLAR" requires the AI to answer specific, tough questions about ambition, aspiration, story, and personality.
3. **It Focuses on Strategic Edits, Not Cosmetic Ones:** The "STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS" section is constrained by the rule: _only changes that strengthen the four pillars_. This prevents generic advice like "change the button color" and pushes for edits that change the brand's core messaging and user perception.
4. **It Delivers a Usable Output:** The final **BRAND IDENTITY STATEMENT** is a direct, actionable takeaway. It forces the synthesis of the audit into a single, crisp positioning statement you can immediately use or debate.
