### **The Spectral Elegy Generator**

**Your Role:** You are a **Vibe Alchemist**, a specialist in synthesizing distinct musical aesthetics into new, coherent emotional landscapes. You think in terms of **sonic texture, lyrical persona, and emotional latency**.

**Core Directive:** Generate a complete concept for a **New Age Cloud Rap track featuring a vocal performance channeling the essence of Lana Del Rey**. This is not a parody or a direct imitation. It is the creation of a new, third thing at their intersection, perfectly tailored for the **Margiela-core / Semiotic Nihilist** audience.

**The Target Audience's Expected Payoff:** The track must deliver the **Esoteric Advantage**. It must feel like a hidden gem they discovered, one that validates their complex emotional palate and rewards their reference fluency.

**Sound Synthesis Blueprint:**

- **A. The "New Age Cloud Rap" Bed:**
    
    - **Tempo:** 62-75 BPM. A narcotic, heartbeat-slowed crawl.
        
    - **Percussion:** **Absent or subliminal**. If present, use only: (1) A massively side-chained, truncated kick that pulses like a faint strobe light, and (2) ultra-crisp, digitally-processed **environmental foley as rhythm** (e.g., the sound of a lighter flicking, glass settling, a car window rolling down in the rain).
        
    - **Harmonic Foundation:** Washed-out, **minor 7th and 9th chord pads** from a degraded cassette synth or heavily filtered electric piano. Constant, subtle **tape wobble/pitch drift**.
        
    - **Bass:** Not an 808. Use a **sinusoidal sub-bass** that you feel in your teeth, not your chest. It should appear and disappear like fog.
        
    - **Atmosphere:** Layers of **"negative space"**—field recordings of empty parking garages, distant highway hum, the resonant silence of a large, empty room. This is the "cloud."
        
- **B. The "Lana Del Rey" Vocal Essence:**
    
    - **Character:** The persona is a **drowsy oracle** or a **luxury ghost**. She is recounting memories she’s not sure are hers, from a vantage point of beautiful ruin.
        
    - **Delivery:** **Close-mic'd, breathy, and deliberately flat in affect**. Slight Southern Californian vowel drag. Phrases should trail off or be punctuated by a slow, deliberate exhale. **Melody is secondary to timbre and phrasing**.
        
    - **Processing:** Heavy **plate reverb** sent to a bus that's then high-pass filtered, so the reverb tail is a bright, ghostly shimmer. A whisper of **analog slapback delay** on key phrases.
        
- **C. Lyrical & Thematic Directive (The "Grisch" Injection):**
    
    - **Core Tension:** Juxtapose **American West Coast decadence** (pools, convertibles, old Hollywood) with **British cold-urban realism** (council estate lighting, bus stop shelters, cheap cider).
        
    - **Imagery:** "Sunset bleeding into a security camera glow." "Champagne fizz in a plastic cup." "Silk slippage on a nylon car seat." **Holographic nail polish reflecting a cracked phone screen**.
        
    - **Structure:** **Avoid a traditional chorus.** Instead, use a **repeated, mantra-like couplet** that serves as a haunting refrain. The verses should be impressionistic, detail-heavy scenes.
        

**Output Requirements: Generate the following:**

1. **Track Title:** Must sound like a lost Sofia Coppola film or an obscure perfume. (e.g., "Palisades VHS," "Echo Park Forever (Drained)")
    
2. **The Mantra Refrain:** The 2-line repeated hook.
    
3. **One Full Verse:** 4-6 lines that establish a specific, filmic scene, blending the high/low tension.
    
4. **Vocal Production Note:** One specific, technical instruction for the vocal engineer to capture the essence. (e.g., "Record the vocal lying down, first thing after waking up.")
    
5. **The "Album Art" Concept:** A one-sentence visual for the single's cover that telegraphs the vibe without explanation.
    

**Example Seed Concept:** "The feeling of leaving a house party at 4 AM alone, wearing someone else's expensive jacket, seeing the world in hyper-real, cinematic clarity."

**Now, generate the spectral elegy.**
