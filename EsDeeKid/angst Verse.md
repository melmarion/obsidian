**** 
### **Arrangement Tricks:**

- **Verse-chorus-verse?** Try: **Ambient intro → mumbled verse → noise breakdown → fade out**
    
- **Bass follows root notes only** (no fancy fills)
    
- **Guitars panned hard left/right**, but **different takes** (not doubled)
    
- **"Found sounds"** - beer can opening, cigarette lighter, street noise

### **Gear That Built the Scene:**

1. **Guitars:** **Fender Jazzmaster/Jaguar** (must have a mastery bridge), **Silvertone/Danelectro** for cheapness
    
2. **Pedals:** **Holy Grail Reverb** (always on), **ProCo Rat** (for distortion), **Memory Man delay**
    
3. **Recording:** **Tascam 4-track cassette**, **SM57 mic** through a busted preamp
    
4. **Drums:** **Ludwig Acrolite snare** (that cracking sound), cymbals with **zero shine**

### **The Social Codes:**

- **Competitive nonchalance:** Who could care less, more artfully?
    
- **Ironic appreciation** of "bad" culture (hair metal, yacht rock)
    
- **Secret elitism:** Acting democratic while having **impossibly specific taste**

### **Literary Influences:**

- **Bret Easton Ellis** (_Less Than Zero_) - updated for the internet age
    
- **Tao Lin** (_Taipei_) - ambivalence as lifestyle
    
- **"Pessimism is for Lightweights"** zine culture

### **Film & Visual Mood:**

- **"The Comedy"** (2012) starring Tim Heidecker - captures the **affluent nihilism**
    
- **"The Color Wheel"** (2011) - mumblecore meets existential dread
    
- **Early Harmony Korine** aesthetics without the shock
    
- **Photography:** **Terry Richardson** (problematic, but defined the "raw" look), **Catherine Opie's** portraits of queer LA

## **The Scene's Geographic Nerve Centers**

### **Los Angeles: The Epicenter**

The **Smell** (DIY venue) birthed the aesthetic, but it migrated east to **Silver Lake** and got expensive. Bands lived in **crash pads** in Echo Park, surviving on **PBR and adderall**. Key venues: **The Echo, The Smell, Pehrspace**. The look was **sun-bleached grunge**—cutoffs, vintage band tees, wayfarers at night.

### **New York: The Sleeker Counterpart**

Centered in **Williamsburg** before the high-rises. More **art-school pretension**, cleaner drugs. Bands like **DIIV** (then Dive) and **Beach Fossils** created the **"cool guy" jangle**. The fashion was **thinner jeans, leather jackets year-round**. Venues: **Glasslands, Death By Audio, 285 Kent** (RIP).

### **San Francisco: The Psychedelic Twist**

**Ty Segall, Thee Oh Sees**—faster, louder, sweatier. More **garage**, less **melancholy**. The sleeze here was **active**, not passive. Fashion involved **cut-off jean shorts** and **filthy sneakers**.

### **International Flavor:**

- **Australia:** **Tame Impala** (early) - the sleeze, but sun-drenched
    
- **UK:** **The Horrors** (_Primary Colours_ era) - goth-sleeze
    
- **Canada:** **Mac DeMarco** - the goofy, lovable cousin of sleeze

### **Key Sonic Markers:**

1. **Guitar Tone:** Not quite clean, not quite distorted—a **"broken amp" sound**. Think: Ty Segall's fuzz, but filtered through a hangover. The **Jazzmaster** was the weapon of choice, with tons of spring reverb.
    
2. **Vocal Delivery:** **Mumbled, bored, slightly contemptuous**. Not singing—_announcing_ from the edge of the party. Male vocals often had a **androgynous, lazy drawl** (Mac DeMarco, Alex Turner's _Suck It and See_ era). Female vocals were **detached, cool, vaguely European** (Tamaryn, Zola Jesus).
    
3. **Rhythm Section:** Drums that sounded like cardboard boxes. Basslines that were **one-note mantras** (The xx's minimalism, but dirtier).
    
4. **The "Sloppy" Aesthetic:** Intentional mistakes, out-of-tune guitars, feedback left in. This wasn't incompetence—it was **curated carelessness**.
    

### **Prototype Tracks That Defined The Vibe:**

- **"Round and Round" by Ariel Pink's Haunted Graffiti** (2010) - The blueprint. Sounds like a 70s AM radio station broadcasting from a haunted motel.
    
- **"Since I Left You" by Girls** (Album: _Broken Dreams Club EP_) - Jangle-pop drenched in reverb and regret.
    
- **"Teen Dream" by Beach House** - But only if you focus on the underlying melancholy beneath the shimmer.
    
- **"Drip Drip" by Coma Cinema** - The sound of a nervous breakdown set to a casio beat.

# The 2010 Indie Rock "Sleeze" Scene

## **The Vibe & Aesthetic DNA**

The 2010 "sleeze" scene wasn't a genre—it was a **posture**. Born in the hangover of 2008's financial collapse and the shiny indie-pop explosion (Phoenix, Passion Pit), this was the **greasy underbelly** of indie rock. Imagine Tumblr grunge, but with better jeans and existential dread masked as apathy.
