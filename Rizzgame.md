# **GAME DESIGN: Social Alchemist**

## **1. GAME TITLE & LOGLINE**

**Title:** **"Frame Exchange"**  
**Logline:** A game where you must **master high-value social navigation** by **crafting dialogue that exchanges emotional validation for behavioral alignment** to achieve **social momentum**.

## **2. THE CORE MECHANIC (The "How You Play")**

**Mechanic:** **"The Value Transaction"**  
Every interaction is a **three-part exchange** where you must:

1. **Acknowledge their position** (emotional cost: minimal)
2. **Introduce your frame** (value offered: perspective shift)
3. **Request specific alignment** (behavioral ask: clear next step)

**The Exchange Rate:** You're trading emotional validation for behavioral compliance. Poor validation = no compliance. Clear value proposition = smooth compliance.

## **3. SCENARIO GENERATOR (The "Content Engine")**

**Challenge Formula:**  
`[THEIR POSITION] + [YOUR DESIRED OUTCOME] + [SOCIAL COST]`

**Generator Components:**

**THEIR POSITION (Emotional State + Status):**
- "Defensive after receiving criticism"
- "Distracted by competing priorities"
- "Skeptical of your proposal's value"
- "Superior in status/seniority"
- "Indifferent to your presence"
- "Testing your boundaries playfully"

**YOUR DESIRED OUTCOME (Behavioral Shift):**
- "Secure 15 minutes of undivided attention"
- "Gain agreement on a controversial point"
- "Redirect focus to your priority"
- "Establish equal footing despite status difference"
- "Create intimacy opportunity"
- "Set a boundary without creating conflict"

**SOCIAL COST (Their Risk/Investment):**
- "Time investment in listening"
- "Ego investment in admitting error"
- "Priority investment in your agenda"
- "Status investment in treating you equally"
- "Vulnerability investment in connection"
- "Certainty investment in trying your way"

## **4. SCORING & FEEDBACK SYSTEM (The "How You Win/Learn")**

**Scoring Criteria (Transaction Efficiency):**

**VALIDATION ACCURACY (0-4 points):**
- +4: Acknowledges their position **without agreeing with it**
- +2: Names the emotion/position correctly
- -2: Invalidates or minimizes their position
- -4: Agrees with their limiting position (loses frame)

**VALUE PROPOSITION (0-4 points):**
- +4: Offers clear benefit for their compliance
- +2: Frames compliance as **their advantage**
- -2: Frames as mutual benefit (weak position)
- -4: Frames as your need (begging position)

**BEHAVIORAL SPECIFICITY (0-4 points):**
- +4: Clear, actionable next step
- +2: Time-bound or context-specific ask
- -2: Vague or open-ended request
- -4: No clear ask (wastes transaction)

**SOCIAL LEVERAGE BONUS:**
- +2: Uses their own values/logic against them
- +3: Makes refusal seem more costly than compliance

**Transaction Feedback:**

After each attempt:
1. **Compliance Probability:** "75% chance of alignment"
2. **Power Delta:** "+2 social credit with this person"
3. **Efficiency Score:** "Traded 2 seconds of validation for 15 minutes of focus"
4. **Failed Transaction Analysis:** (if under 8 points) "You validated too much → lost negotiating position"

**Loss Condition:**  
Three consecutive **inefficient transactions** (below 6 points) triggers **"Social Capital Drain"** — the person begins to see your requests as low-priority or burdensome. You must recover by crafting a **high-value renegotiation**.

## **5. PROGRESSION CYCLE (The "Levels")**

**Level 1: "Basic Exchanges"**
- **Goal:** Master clear value propositions
- **Rule Set:** Their position is straightforward, desired outcome is reasonable, social cost is low
- **Example:** *"Colleague is busy but needs to review your document. Goal: Get feedback by EOD."*

**Level 2: "Hostile Negotiations"**
- **Goal:** Extract compliance from resistant positions
- **Complication:** **"Negative Social Capital"** — They start with reason to dislike/distrust you
- **Example:** *"Person you previously criticized is defensive. Goal: Get them to implement your suggestion anyway."*

**Level 3: "Asymmetric Power Games"**
- **Goal:** Get compliance from superiors without formal authority
- **Master Challenge:** **"The Invisible Ask"** — You cannot make direct requests. Must engineer situations where compliance appears to be their idea.
- **Example:** *"CEO who dismisses junior staff input. Goal: Get them to adopt your strategy while believing it was theirs."*

## **6. MODULAR DIALOGUE SYSTEM**

**VALIDATION BANK (Precise, not pandering):**
- "I understand why you'd prioritize X given your position."
- "Your skepticism makes sense based on past experiences."
- "If I were in your role, I'd have similar concerns."
- "You're right to focus on Y first."
- "That perspective is valuable context."

**VALUE PROPOSITION BANK (Clear exchange rates):**
- "What you might find valuable here is..."
- "The reason this benefits your priorities specifically is..."
- "This actually addresses the concern you just raised about..."
- "Here's how this saves you time on Z later..."
- "This creates an opportunity you mentioned wanting..."

**BEHAVIORAL ASK BANK (Specific, bounded requests):**
- "What I need from you is 90 seconds on this specific point."
- "The next step that would unblock this is your approval on X."
- "If you could clarify one thing about Y, I can handle the rest."
- "Your input on A versus B would determine our direction."
- "A quick yes/no on this would save us both time."

# **GAME REDESIGN: Empathy Lens**

## **1. GAME TITLE & LOGLINE**

**Title:** **"Needfinder"**  
**Logline:** A game where you must **diagnose a man's unspoken core need in real-time** by **observing behavior patterns and testing response effectiveness** to achieve **emotional resonance**.

## **2. THE CORE MECHANIC (The "How You Play")**

**Mechanic:** **"The Diagnostic Loop"**  
Each round presents a **behavioral pattern** (not a single moment). You must:

1. **OBSERVE** (Identify the pattern from 3+ data points)
2. **HYPOTHESIZE** (Select the most likely **core need** from the 7: Safety/Appreciation/Acceptance/Belief/Respect/Muse/Encouragement)
3. **TEST** (Deploy one **diagnostic response** to confirm your hypothesis)
4. **ADJUST** (Based on his reaction, confirm or change your diagnosis)

**The Twist:** You don't get points for "fixing" anything. You get points for **accurately identifying the need**. The dialogue is merely your diagnostic tool.

## **3. SCENARIO GENERATOR (The "Content Engine")**

**Pattern Formula:**  
`[PATTERN TYPE] over [TIMEFRAME] with [TRIGGER/RELIEF] data`

**Generator Components:**

**PATTERN TYPES (The 7 Needs manifested):**
- **Withdrawal-After-Criticism Pattern** (Safety)
- **Effort-Followed-by-Seeking-Validation Pattern** (Appreciation) 
- **Self-Deprecation-Then-Testing Pattern** (Acceptance)
- **Task-Avoidance-After-Micromanagement Pattern** (Belief)
- **Boundary-Pushing-Then-Observing Pattern** (Respect)
- **Excitement-Followed-by-Disengagement Pattern** (Muse)
- **Challenge-Attempt-Then-Diminishment Pattern** (Encouragement)

**TIMEFRAMES:**
- "Over the last week"
- "Since your argument on Tuesday"
- "During this weekend project"
- "Since he started his new job"
- "Throughout your vacation planning"

**TRIGGER/RELIEF DATA:**
- "He lights up when you ______ but withdraws when you ______"
- "He repeatedly mentions ______ but changes topic when you ______"
- "He started ______ after you ______ and stopped when you ______"

## **4. SCORING & FEEDBACK SYSTEM**

**Scoring Criteria:**

**PATTERN RECOGNITION (0-5 points):**
- +5: Identifies the correct **core need** based on behavioral pattern
- +3: Identifies correct **category** (e.g., "ego need" vs "safety need")
- +1: Identifies surface emotion only ("he's upset")
- -2: Misinterprets pattern (e.g., calls need for Respect "need for Control")

**DIAGNOSTIC RESPONSE QUALITY (0-5 points):**
- +5: Response **tests for the specific need** without addressing content
- +3: Response addresses the need generally
- +1: Response addresses surface behavior only
- -3: Response **contradicts the hypothesized need** (e.g., giving advice when testing for Belief)

**FEEDBACK LOOP ACCURACY (0-5 points):**
- +5: Correctly interprets his response to your diagnostic
- +3: Adjusts hypothesis based on new data
- -2: Clings to initial hypothesis despite contradictory evidence

**Perfect Round = 15 points**

**Feedback Format:**
After each round:
1. **Need Reveal:** "Core Need: BELIEF IN COMPETENCE"
2. **Pattern Analysis:** "Withdrawal after task interference → seeking autonomy → needing trust"
3. **Diagnostic Efficacy:** "Your question about his approach confirmed the need"
4. **Missed Clues:** (if missed) "You overlooked his mention of past successes"

## **5. PROGRESSION CYCLE**

**Level 1: "Clear Signals"**
- Patterns are obvious, 1:1 with needs
- **Example:** *"He cooked dinner three times this week and each time asked if you noticed."* (Appreciation)

**Level 2: "Mixed Patterns"**
- **Complication:** Two needs present simultaneously, one primary
- **Example:** *"He's working late (Appreciation through effort) but defensive when asked about it (Safety from criticism)."*

**Level 3: "Need Stacking"**
- **Master Challenge:** Needs change **mid-interaction** based on your response
- **Example:** *Starts seeking Encouragement → receives it poorly (feels pitied) → shifts to needing Respect instead.*

## **6. DIAGNOSTIC RESPONSE BANK**

**For Testing SAFETY:**
- "I want to understand, not judge. Walk me through your thinking."
- "However you feel about this is completely valid here."

**For Testing APPRECIATION:**
- "I noticed you put effort into X. Tell me about your process."
- "What part of that are you most proud of?"

**For Testing ACCEPTANCE:**
- "I appreciate how you handle Y, even when it's challenging."
- "What's your natural approach to this, before any adjustments?"

**For Testing BELIEF:**
- "How would you solve this if I weren't here?"
- "I trust your judgment on this next step."

**For Testing RESPECT:**
- "Your concern about Z makes sense. How would you prefer we handle it?"
- "What boundaries would make this work for you?"

**For Testing MUSE:**
- "When you're in your element with A, what does that feel like?"
- "What inspires you about this?"

**For Testing ENCOURAGEMENT:**
- "Remember when you succeeded with B against the odds?"
- "What's one small win you could claim here?"

## **7. THE "PLAY NOW" SEED**

**Scenario 1 (Clear Signal):**
*Pattern: He's redone the same home repair three times this week. Each time he shows you, he points out flaws before you can comment. When you offered to help yesterday, he said "I've got it" sharply.*
**Your Diagnostic:** Which need is being tested? What one question would confirm it?

**Scenario 2 (Mixed Pattern):**
*Pattern: He's been talking excitedly about a career opportunity (detailed plans, researching). But when you ask specifics about timeline, he changes subject to your weekend plans. When you circled back, he said "It probably won't happen anyway."*
**Your Diagnostic:** Primary need vs. protective need?

**Scenario 3 (Need Stacking):**
*Pattern: He failed at a personal goal. First he made self-deprecating jokes (testing Acceptance). You laughed along. He then became quiet. You asked if he wanted advice. He said "No, I just need to try harder" (new need emerging).*
**Your Diagnostic:** What need just emerged? Why did your response trigger the shift?
