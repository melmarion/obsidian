# Old money culture and behavior:

1. "The Proper Bostonians" by Cleveland Amory

   - This book offers in-depth insights into the formation and development of old money families in Boston, including their history, traditions, values, and attitudes.

2. "The Rich and the Super-Rich: A Study in the Power of Money Today" by Ferdinand Lundberg

   - This book delves into the dynamics and power structures within the old money elite, providing valuable context on the nuances of their social and cultural norms.

3. "The Gilded Age" by Mark Twain and Charles Dudley Warner

   - As a classic work of American literature, this novel explores the tensions between old money and new money in 19th century society, offering Mary a glimpse into the historical roots of these class divisions.

4. "The Great Gatsby" by F. Scott Fitzgerald

   - This iconic novel provides a scathing critique of the old money elite, which Mary could use to better understand their mentality and behaviors, as well as how to navigate their social circles.

1. Emphasis on Modesty and Refinement:

   - Old money families traditionally prioritize modesty over ostentatious displays of wealth, as well as refinement and restraint over brashness or vulgarity.

2. Appreciation for History and Tradition:

   - Old money families often place great importance on their family's history and the traditions that have been passed down through generations.

3. Understanding of Noblesse Oblige:

   - The concept of noblesse oblige, or the obligation of the nobility to be generous and responsible to those less fortunate, is deeply ingrained in old money culture.

4. Familiarity with Exclusive Social Circles:

   - Old money families maintain tight-knit, exclusive social circles, with a keen awareness of who belongs and who does not.
