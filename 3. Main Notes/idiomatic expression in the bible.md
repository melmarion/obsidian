# Idioms

1. "Let me catch my breath." Meaning: "give me some time." This comes from Job 9:18.

2. "A drop in the bucket." Meaning: "a very small, insignificant amount." This comes from Isaiah 40:15.

4. "The apple of one's eye." Meaning: "something very dear." This comes from Psalm 17:8.

5. "Know." Used as an idiom for "sexual intercourse." Examples are in Genesis 4:1 and Matthew 1:25.

6. "Seed." Used as an idiom for "children" or "descendants." Example in Genesis 22:17.

7. "The manner of women." An idiom for a woman's menstrual period, as in Genesis 31:35.

8. "Flowing with milk and honey." An idiom communicating that the Promised Land was "fertile."

9. "Melting heart." An idiom meaning "losing courage," as in Deuteronomy 20:8.  

10. "One who urinates on a wall." An idiom referring to a "male," as in 1 Samuel 25:22.

11. "Cover one's feet." An idiom thought to mean "relieve oneself," as in Judges 3:24 and 1 Samuel 24:3.

12. "From Dan to Beersheba." An idiom meaning "all of Israel," as in 2 Samuel 3:10 and 1 Kings 4:25.

13. "Great before God." A literal translation of an idiom meaning "highly regarded."
