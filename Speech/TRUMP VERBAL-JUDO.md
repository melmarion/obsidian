Each tactic is written as a **micro-script**: the exact words, the timing, and the psychological lever it yanks.

# TRUMP VERBAL-JUDO SET-LIST  
*(arranged in the order he usually fires them)*

---

### 0. PRE-FIGHT CHECK (do this in your head, 0.5 s)
1. **Label the emotion** you see: *“That’s anger”*  
2. **Decide the outcome** you want: *“Audience laughs at him, not me”*  
3. **Pick the tactic # below** → fire.

---

### 1. The Interrupt-Ambush
**When**  
Opponent is mid-sentence, building steam.

**Script**  
> “Excuse-me—excuse-me—**wrong**.” *(staccato, 3-beat, louder each beat)*

**Psychology**  
Breaks their auditory pattern → resets crowd attention to you.

**Visual**  
Hand chop + lean forward 5°.

---

### 2. The One-Word Reframe
**When**  
You need to dump a negative label on them *forever*.

**Script**  
> “**Low-energy** Jeb.”  
> “**Crooked** Hillary.”  
> “**Sleepy** Joe.”

**Formula**  
[1 adjective with hard consonant] + [First-Name]  
Test: must force opponent to *explain* → they lose 8 s of air-time.

---

### 3. High-Ground Hijack
**When**  
Policy details are drowning you.

**Script**  
> “Look, we all want **clean water**, right? So let’s talk **real safety**.”

**Lever**  
Moves debate to value no one can boo → audience nods *with* you.

---

### 4. The Boomerang Question
**When**  
Hostile reporter tries to corner you.

**Script**  
> “You’re CNN—**fake news**—why should anyone trust *your* numbers?”

**Effect**  
Puts *them* on trial; crowd now judges the *questioner*, not you.

---

### 5. Authority Flash
**When**  
Credentials are challenged.

**Script**  
> “I’ve been on **the cover of Time** more than anyone—I think I know a little about covers, OK?”

**Prep**  
Have 3 quantified brags memorized (magazine covers, ratings, dollar amounts).

---

### 6. Social-Proof Snowball
**When**  
You sense doubt in the room.

**Script**  
> “Everybody knows it—**ask any pollster**—biggest crowds **in history**.”

**Key**  
Say *“everybody”*, not *“many people”*; absolutes shut down follow-up.

---

### 7. Future-Pace Shutdown
**When**  
Opponent demands specifics you don’t want to give.

**Script**  
> “We’re gonna see **in two weeks**—it’s gonna be **beautiful**, you’ll see.”

**Why it works**  
Thinking past the sale → audience imagines outcome already exists.

---

### 8. Humor Kill-Shot
**When**  
Crowd is tense; you need a laugh *at* opponent’s expense.

**Script** *(2016 debate)*  
> “Now I know why **she’s home**—she got **Schlonged** by Obama in ’08.”

**Rules**  
- One crude word max → laughter + shock  
- Deliver with smile → shields from “mean” label

---

### 9. Empathy-Then-Redirect (rare defensive move)
**When**  
You must absorb punch without looking weak.

**Script**  
> “I **understand** you’re upset—**a lot of people are upset**—that’s why we’re gonna **fix it**.”

**Sequence**  
Feel-Felt-Find in 3 seconds → back to offense.

---

### 10. The “Wanna-Bet” Reversal
**When**  
Opponent says *“You can’t do that!”*

**Script**  
> “**Watch me.**” *(drop mic, move on)*

**Caution**  
Only use if you **already have the power** to act; otherwise you look impotent.

---

### 11. Story-Snipe (Anchoring)
**When**  
You need to plant a *visual* that sticks.

**Script**  
> “They’re **cutting babies** after birth—**that’s not abortion, that’s execution**.”

**Checklist**  
- 10-second mini-story  
- Vivid noun (*babies, sharks, cages*)  
- Moral adjective (*execution, invasion, steal*)

---

### 12. Numeric Sledgehammer
**When**  
You want to sound precise (even if you’re not).

**Script**  
> “$500 **billion**—not million, **billion**—ripped off.”

**Tip**  
Always say the big number **twice**, second time louder.

---

### 13. Compliment-Slice
**When**  
You need to *appear* gracious while twisting knife.

**Script**  
> “Bernie’s **a good guy**—but he **folded like a dog**, so sad.”

**Structure**  
[Praise] + [but] + [animal metaphor] → negates praise, sticks insult.

---

### 14. Repeat-Until-True
**When**  
Fact-checkers call you out.

**Script**  
> “No collusion—**no collusion**—**NO collusion**.” *(triple cadence, fist pump on third)*

**Science**  
Repetition > 3× bypasses analytical brain → enters memory as “truth.”

---

### 15. Exit-Line Echo
**When**  
You need the **last word** on camera.

**Script**  
> “**End of story—everybody knows it.**” *(turn head away = conversation closed)*

**Body**  
Step back 1 foot → physical period.

2. Mirror-check **facial delivery**—half-smile on humor, dead-serious on numeric.  
3. Practice **hand-chop** timing: word = impact, chop = visual exclamation.  
4. End every drill with **Exit-Line Echo** → train your brain to *close*.
