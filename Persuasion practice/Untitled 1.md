**Critique of Travel as Status**
People idolize spending money on travel for the experience, but it is just an indirect way of flaunting wealth under the name of gaining knowledge. It implies "I am smarter and better than you because I do not spend on frivolous things." I learned that I do not care about that.

**The Real Value: Meeting New People**
The most interesting thing about going somewhere new is meeting people you did not have access to before. I have the most fun in airports than in actually travelling and thinking of an itinerary to take pictures and prove to the world you went somewhere other people cannot afford. It is a status game with more stress.

**Rejecting Recycled Anti-American Opinions**
People try to be high and mighty by implying they are different from other Americans, saying they hate them because they are rude, obnoxious, and dirty. Those are just recycled opinions by people who dissociate themselves with what America represents in its aggressiveness. If I am honest with myself, I love Americans. I love the frontier mindset, I love how they are more open to talk to strangers, how open-minded they are with a person's differences. Those qualities make it easier to talk to strangers and open the gates of nepotism and getting opportunities you would not have in a meritocracy.

**The Sameness of Scenery**
The second reason people travel is for the scenery. If we are honest with ourselves, we would admit that most of the world is the same. Every place has mountains, desert, ocean. They all look the same at the end of the day, especially if you are from a place with eco-diversity. There are few exceptions that are more extreme in one type of terrain.

**Travel as a Materialist Guise**
Travel is a waste of time and resources under the guise of what it does not represent, which is anti-materialism.
