    - **The Status Layer:** (Who's up, who's down). This is about saving face, projecting confidence, and undermining the opponent's social standing.

**Mechanic Specification: The Street Level Systems**

- **Core Resources:**
    
    - **Influence (Your Win Condition):** The collective buy-in from the audience (or the opponent's own psyche). Get it to 100% to win.
        
    - **Composure (Your Stamina):** Your mental/emotional stamina. When it hits zero, you **Crack** and blurt out something that loses massive Influence (e.g., an insult, a desperate plea).

        - _"Steve Jobs' Reality Distortion" (Confidence):_ Temporarily ignore current "Facts" damage and project an inevitable future where you're right.
            
    - **Status Plays:** Attack or defend social position.
        
        - _Trump's _"Actually, That's Good" (Reframe Spin)_: This models the **narrative jiu-jitsu** of taking a weakness or attack and reframing it as evidence of your unique strength, authenticity, or as an elite persecution.         - _"Graceful Concession" (Face-Save):_ Voluntarily lose a small point on the Facts Layer to gain Influence and Composure. "You're right about that detail, which shows you're smart, so you'll see my bigger point..."

    - **Emotional Triggers:** Bypass facts entirely.
        
        - _"Appeal to Tribal Identity":_ If Social Weather is "Polarized," this card steals a massive amount of Influence from opponents not in your perceived "group."
            
        - _"Gaslighting Gambit":_ Does damage directly to the opponent's _Composure_ by forcing them to question their memory/interpretation. "That's not what you said..."
            
        - _"Viral Anecdote":_ Overwhelms one piece of statistical "Fact" with a single, emotionally charged story.
            
- **Opponent: The Low-Level Debater Profile**
    
    - **Primary Drive:** **Emotional Security & Ego Defense.** They don't care about truth; they care about not feeling stupid or losing face.
        
    - **Strategy:** Spams **Emotional Triggers** and low-cost **Status Plays** (like mockery). Their "Facts" are weak and often self-contradictory.
        
    - **Critical Weakness:** **The Need for Consistency.** They can be trapped by their own past statements. Playing a **"Gotcha Hypocrisy"** card (if you've logged their contradiction) can shatter their Composure.

It's **"Off-Ramp"**: "Look, I think you're getting upset because you actually care a lot about this, which I respect. We probably agree on the big picture." This grants them a dignified exit, steals all remaining Influence, and wins you the encounter.

### **Prompt: Design the Debate Roguelite "Face Saver"**

**Your Role:** You are the **Lead Designer of Klei Entertainment**. Following the critical and commercial success of *Griftlands*, you have been given the greenlight for a bold spin-off: **"Face Saver."** Your mandate is to strip out all physical combat and double down on the negotiation system, transforming it into a deep, strategic **debate roguelite**. **Core Game Pillars:**
1.  **Pure Persuasion:** All conflict is resolved through structured debate. "Health" is **Credibility**. Lose it all, and you're rhetorically defeated (you lose face, the deal collapses, you get thrown out).
2.  **Deck-Building as Argument Crafting:** Players build a deck not of attacks, but of **Argument Cards** (Claims, Evidence, Rebuttals) and **Tactic Cards** (Fallacies, Diplomacy, Diversions).
3.  **Procedural Social Worlds:** Each run is a journey through a vibrant, procedurally-generated social arena (like a corporate boardroom, a political convention, a village council). "Enemies" are debate opponents with unique personalities, agendas, and rhetorical styles.
4.  **Stealth Education:** Game mechanics are built directly on real-world concepts. Playing well *means* understanding and applying/countering logical fallacies, rhetorical devices, and face-saving social maneuvers.

**Mechanic Specification:**
You must design the core **Debate Loop** to mirror *Griftlands* but be wholly original. Use this framework:

*   **The Two Tracks:** Each debater has two resources:
    *   **Logic Meter (Public):** The overt score. Reduce your opponent's to 0 to win the point. Filled by playing **Claim** and **Evidence** cards.
    *   **Composure Meter (Hidden/Personal):** Your mental stamina. Playing powerful cards or being hit by sharp rebuttals drains it. If it hits zero, you become **Flustered**, applying negative status effects (e.g., "Ad Hominem Prone," "Repetitive").
*   **The Card Archetypes:**
    *   **Claims:** Foundation cards. Low impact, build your Logic position. (e.g., "Basic Assertion," "Statistical Trend").
    *   **Evidence:** Played to bolster a Claim, multiplying its Logic damage. Requires setup. (e.g., "Anecdotal Example," "Peer-Reviewed Study").
    *   **Rebuttals:** Instant-speed cards that counter opponent's plays. The core of interaction. (e.g., "Spot Contradiction," "Question Methodology").
    *   **Tactics (The "Fun" Layer):** These introduce fallacies and social moves.
        *   **Offensive Tactics:** Apply a fallacy to opponent's argument to bypass Logic. (e.g., **"Straw Man"** (Deal damage ignoring their Evidence), **"Appeal to Emotion"** (Drain their Composure)).
        *   **Defensive/Status Tactics:** Protect yourself or set up future plays. (e.g., **"Save Face"** (Transfer Composure loss to Logic loss), **"Gish Gallop"** (Play multiple low-value Claims at once)).
*   **Opponent Design:** Opponents are defined by their **Argument Deck** and their **Rhetorical Bias**.
    *   *The Demagogue:* Heavy use of **Appeal to Emotion** and **Bandwagon**. Weak to **Spot Contradiction**.
    *   *The Pedant:* Relies on **Literal Interpretation** and **Proof by Intimidation**. Weak to **Reductio ad Absurdum**.
    *   *The Gaslighter:* Uses **Moving the Goalposts** and **Whataboutism**. Weak to **Holding Frame** cards.
