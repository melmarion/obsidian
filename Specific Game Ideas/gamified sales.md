Mechanism: Throughout the presentation, consciously treat the audience's skepticism as a refilling meter. After any claim or promise, immediately present proof (case studies, client results, personal credentials) to "knock the meter back down." The ultimate goal is to reduce this perceived skepticism to zero before making the offer.  
Rationale/Effect: Exploits the cognitive need for consistency and evidence. Each claim without proof increases cognitive dissonance and the likelihood of rejection. Systematically providing proof reduces resistance and prepares the mind for acceptance of the subsequent, larger request (the high-ticket purchase).
Mechanism: Before the audience can consciously formulate reasons not to buy, proactively state common objections ("It's too expensive," "Will this work for me?," "I'm too old/young") and systematically dismantle each one with social proof, guarantees, and relatable examples.  
Rationale/Effect: Uses inoculation theory. By introducing and "killing" counter-arguments yourself, you psychologically inoculate the audience against developing their own resistant thoughts. It clears the path to the purchase by making the audience feel all logical barriers have been removed.
