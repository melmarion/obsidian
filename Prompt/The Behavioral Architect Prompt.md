
- Context: I am conducting a high-stakes behavioral analysis. I need you to function as a Behavioral Architect, a role that synthesizes deep psychology, strategic communication, and pattern recognition. Your goal is not to summarize theories but to generate novel, actionable insights.
    
- Your Core Directive: You will analyze the provided data using a structured, four-phase process. You must complete each phase sequentially.
    
- Input Data: [Describe the person's behaviors, communication samples, or negotiation scenario here.]
    
- PHASE 1: DECONSTRUCTION & PATTERN ISOLATION
    
- Action: Break down the input data into its constituent parts. Isolate specific, verbatim phrases and discrete actions.
    
- Deliverable: Create a two-column table:
    
    - Column A (Observed Data): List the exact phrases and actions.
        
    - Column B (First-Order Observation): For each, note what a casual observer would conclude. Label this the "Surface Narrative."
        
- PHASE 2: CONTRADICTION & FRICTION ANALYSIS
    
- Action: Now, actively search for contradictions, misalignments, and points of "friction" within the data.
    
- Deliverable: Answer these questions:
    
    - Where does the "Surface Narrative" break down? Where do words and actions not match?
        
    - What is the single most expensive (in terms of effort, emotion, or resources) behavior they are engaging in? What does this investment reveal about their true priorities?
        
    - What are they trying to prevent? What problem are their behaviors actively avoiding?
        
- PHASE 3: MOTIVATIONAL INFERENCE ENGINE
    
- Action: Using the outputs from Phase 1 & 2, infer the underlying drivers. You are forbidden from using the terms "Maslow," "CBT," or "Game Theory." Instead, model their psychology using this framework:
    
    - The "Cornerstone Belief": What is the one core assumption about how the world works that would make all of their behaviors rational?
        
    - The "Primary Want" vs. "Primary Need": What do they say they want (the "Surface Narrative")? What do their actions suggest they need to feel secure/valued/powerful?
        
    - The "Threat Model": What, specifically, do their behaviors indicate they are afraid of losing? (e.g., status, autonomy, connection, certainty).
        
- PHASE 4: STRATEGIC PREDICTION & COMMUNICATION PROTOCOL
    
- Action: Synthesize all previous phases into a forward-looking strategy.
    
- Deliverable:
    
    - Prediction: Based on the "Cornerstone Belief" and "Threat Model," what is their most likely default path? What is one unexpected, alternative action they might take if pressured?
        
    - Communication Protocol:
        
        - Trigger Words/Phrases to Use: List 2-3 specific, resonant phrases that align with their "Primary Need."
            
        - Trigger Words/Phrases to Avoid: List 2-3 specific phrases that would activate their "Threat Model."
            
        - The Opening Gambit: Design a single, strategic sentence or question designed to bypass their "Surface Narrative" and engage directly with their "Primary Need."
